How Do We Protect Your Information?
At Sanjog Matrimony, we implement robust measures to protect your personal information and maintain your privacy. Here’s how we safeguard your data:
  •           Data Security: We use industry-standard encryption technologies (such as SSL) to protect data transmitted between your device and our servers. This helps prevent unauthorized access, alteration, or disclosure of your information.
  • Access Control: Access to your personal information is restricted to authorized personnel who need it to perform their job responsibilities. We regularly review access permissions and enhance controls as necessary.
  • Data Minimization: We collect only the minimum amount of personal information necessary to provide our services effectively. We do not retain your information longer than required for the purposes outlined in our Privacy Policy.
  • Third-Party Security: We carefully select and vet third-party service providers who may have access to your information. We ensure they adhere to high security standards and contractual obligations regarding data protection.
  • Regular Audits and Monitoring: We conduct regular security audits and vulnerability assessments of our systems and infrastructure. This helps us identify and address potential security risks promptly.
  •       User Authentication: We employ strong user authentication mechanisms, such as passwords and multi-factor authentication, to ensure only authorized users can access their accounts.
  •       Training and Awareness: We provide regular training to our staff on data protection practices and the importance of safeguarding user information. This includes raising awareness about potential security threats and best practices for data handling.
  •       Incident Response: In the event of a data breach or security incident, we have procedures in place to respond promptly, mitigate any potential impact, and notify affected users as required by law.
  •       Legal Compliance: We comply with applicable data protection laws and regulations, including the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA), and others relevant to our operations.
  •       User Education: We empower users with information and tools to help them protect their own privacy, such as providing options for account security settings and privacy preferences.
